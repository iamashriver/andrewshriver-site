Case Study: Building and Scaling a Global UX Practice

Project: From Zero to Global UX: Building a Scalable Design Organization
Project Duration: 2021-Present
Company: Janus Henderson Investors

Scroll Down
  • LinkedIn

Overview

When I joined Janus Henderson in 2021, there was no formal UX function. My mandate was to build a global UX capability, establish repeatable processes, and elevate the firm’s digital experience.

At a Glance

Problem

  • No centralized UX team or practice
  • Inconsistent design standards across regions and channels
  • Limited user research and testing
  • Fragmented tools and workflows
  • UX viewed as a service, not a strategic partner

Approach

  • Team formation: Hired and scaled a global UX team across regions
  • Process design: Established a formal UX workflow, engagement model, and SLA
  • Design system: Created and evolved a comprehensive design system and digital style guide
  • Tooling transformation: Migrated the organization to Figma, consolidating fragmented tools into a single platform
  • Research foundation: Introduced user testing and built a structured UX research methodology
  • Stakeholder alignment: Led UX education sessions and positioned UX as a strategic partner

Impact

  • Established a scalable global UX practice from the ground up
  • Improved consistency and efficiency through a centralized design system
  • Enabled faster delivery and reduced redundant design work
  • Shifted UX perception from executional to strategic
  • Built strong cross-functional alignment across product, engineering, and marketing

Leadership

This work required more than design; it required organizational change. I led through influence, education, and system thinking, building trust across stakeholders and creating a foundation that continues to scale today.
